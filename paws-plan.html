Plan Overview

A New Way
Forward

The PAWS Plan — People At Work Succeeding — represents the result of an extensive planning process by the Ad Hoc Group, guided by the principles of the prestigious Scanlon Plan, named after Joseph Scanlon of the United Steel Workers and M.I.T., considered the "father of participative management."

PAWS provides the intellectual framework to tap the resources of all individuals at Limerick Veterinary Hospital as a means toward continuously improving personal competence and overall business performance — and, as a result, sharing in the financial and spiritual gains derived from our collective efforts.

The plan empowers every employee to monitor the hospital's performance from within, as opposed to top-down management. As the hospital prospers, so do its people through earnings, abilities, and motivation.

The Scanlon Process is not a quick fix. It handles change — which is inevitable — by empowering the employee who knows their job better than anyone else to do the things that will make the job easier and better.

— Dr. Charles Koenig, President

The Framework

Four
Principles

The PAWS Plan rests on four major principles drawn directly from the Scanlon framework. Together, they form a complete approach to participative management — each pillar reinforcing the others.

01 / PILLAR
Identity &
Education

Identifying and educating ourselves on who our customers, investors, and employees are — and understanding their needs within the business reality.

02 / PILLAR
Participation &
Responsibility

Management's and employees' opportunity and responsibility to influence the decisions that impact their areas of competence — with full accountability.

03 / PILLAR
Equity &
Accountability

Balanced return to employees, customers, and investors alike. Shared accountability in the future development of the work environment through gainsharing.

04 / PILLAR
Competence &
Commitment

Investors, employees, and customers must respond to the constant demand for improvement and change — building an ever-stronger organization together.

Our Story

Where We
Came From

1946
Founded by Dr. Warren B. Rawlings

Limerick Veterinary Hospital was established after Dr. Rawlings retired from Ciba Laboratories. The Limerick area was rural, and the practice was well-suited to large animals — growing to 75% large animal, 25% small animal, anchored by two major dairy herds.

1961
Dr. Charles Koenig Joins the Practice

Dr. Koenig entered the four-room facility and Dr. Rawlings retired. The area was shifting from farming to suburban living, and Dr. Koenig was making 8–10 large animal calls daily — beginning the hospital's long evolution.

1970s–80s
Growth, Technology & New Partners

The hospital expanded with new veterinarians, modern anesthesia equipment, and a growing focus on small animals. The era saw the rise of the veterinary technician — a transformation Limerick embraced fully — and growing importance of the human–animal bond.

1996
Dr. Robert Nicol Joins as Partner

Large animal services were discontinued as small animal care grew. Dr. Nicol joined from Great Britain, bringing orthopedic and surgical expertise, and became a 30% partner in the practice.

1997–1999
The Scanlon Journey Begins

Guided by Ray Terwilliger & Associates, the hospital undertook the full Scanlon certification process — developing job descriptions, timely reviews, Core Group meetings, and the Ad Hoc Committee that became the PAWS Team. On May 21, 1999, employees voted to implement the PAWS Plan.

The combined improvements create a pool of $12,500. After a 50/50 hospital/employee split and a 20% reserve, employees receive $5,000 — equal to 4.16% of quarterly salary on $120,000 eligible payroll. An additional 20% reduction applies if Quality of Service or Suggestion/Participation goals are not met.

The Suggestion Process

W1
Department Meeting

Each department meets and submits at least one good suggestion. Department head retains copies of all suggestions submitted.

W2
Suggestion Committee Review

Committee members receive, review, and research all suggestions. Suggestees may attend to present their idea directly.

W3
Committee Vote

After research, committee members vote on all suggestions. The facilitator compiles findings to bring to the Core Group.

W4
Core Group Decision

The Core Group makes final decisions and provides feedback. Approved suggestions are posted on the PAWS Board with implementation timelines.

Where We're Going

Challenges &
Opportunities

Procedural
Staff Development

Promoting the head technician to a total leadership position, developing composite employee handbooks, standardized salary scales, and improving quarterly review processes.

Personnel
Retention & Growth

Keeping valued employees and attracting new talent. Continuing to invest in veterinary continuing education — giving every team member the best opportunities in their field of interest.

Physical
Technology & Expansion

Implementing a new computer system and constructing a major addition to the hospital and Happy Tails Activity Center — with employee input shaping both decisions.

External
Corporate Competition

Preparing for potential competition from corporate veterinary chains like VCA. PAWS gives LVH the organizational strength to compete on culture, service quality, and community connection.

Client Relations
Communication

Communicating more effectively with pet owners. Establishing life cycle packages for older patients, improving client education, and strengthening long-term relationships.

Industry Trends
Vaccine Protocol Changes

Adapting to evolving vaccination schedules. Each annual visit remains a valuable opportunity — 15 minutes to educate clients, build trust, and demonstrate the full scope of expert veterinary care.
