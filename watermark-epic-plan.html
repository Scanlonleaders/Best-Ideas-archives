Watermark Credit Union
EPIC Plan

A fully realized Scanlon-based gainsharing and participation program adopted in 2005, built around four interconnected principles.

✦
Scanlon / Frost Plan · Financial Services · 2005

Watermark Credit Union (formerly Seattle Telco FCU) developed the EPIC Plan — standing for Equity, Participation, Identity, and Competence — as their route to becoming a billion-dollar credit union. Designed by an elected and appointed team of employees, it represents a textbook application of Scanlon principles in a service-sector organization. Each principle works in concert to create a culture of shared ownership, continuous improvement, and financial transparency.

The Four Principles

I

Principle One

Identity

"Identity is the process of knowing ourselves — where we've come from, where we are now, and where we're going."

The Identity Principle ensures every employee understands the Credit Union's history, mission, competitive landscape, and strategic direction. Informed employees can align their daily work with organizational goals.

  • History from 1938 founding as Seattle Telco FCU
  • Strategic goal: $1 billion in assets by 2012
  • Board structure, duties of care, loyalty & obedience
  • Why the Expense-to-Asset Ratio must improve
  • Capital's impact on members, employees & the CU
Learn More ▾

Why Identity Matters

Watermark's Strategic Plan called for growing assets to $1 billion with a 9% Capital Ratio by year-end 2012 — requiring 12% average annual growth. That meant five new Puget Sound branches, membership growing past 100,000, and staff expanding from 160 to ~260. Without every employee understanding this context, suggestions and decisions lack direction.

Business Literacy Focus

The plan dedicated training resources to making employees "business literate" — understanding the Expense-to-Asset Ratio (historically over 4% vs. an industry average of 3.5%), how capital is built and lost, and how their individual actions affect the Credit Union's long-term viability.

Organizational Context

Credit unions differ from banks: members are both owners and customers. This dual role is central to Watermark's equity model and gives the Identity Principle its cooperative character.

P

Principle Two

Participation

"The opportunity, which only management can give, and the responsibility, which only employees can accept, to influence decisions in their areas of competence."

The Participation Principle creates a structured system for employees to submit suggestions, serve on committees, and drive change — harnessing the untapped knowledge of every staff member.

  • Department-level Review Committees (2–5 elected members)
  • Organization-wide Screening Committee
  • Department & Watermark Improvement Teams (DITs & WITs)
  • Formal Suggestion Process with business-case requirements
  • EPIC Coordinator role (20+ hrs/week dedicated position)
Learn More ▾

Committee Structure

Each department/branch has a Review Committee, led by the manager and staffed by 2–5 elected employees on one-year terms. These committees evaluate suggestions within their scope, charter Department Improvement Teams, and elect a representative to the Screening Committee.

The Screening Committee

A mixed body of elected and appointed members (appointed members can never outnumber elected), the Screening Committee handles cross-functional suggestions, charters Watermark Improvement Teams, approves budgets, and ensures the organization's resources are directed at its highest-impact opportunities.

Suggestion Types Encouraged

  • Increase operational efficiencies or reduce expenses
  • Increase revenue or member service quality
  • Improve productivity, quality, or employee morale

Response Options

Every suggestion receives a formal response: Approved, Queued (approved but awaiting resources), Forwarded (sent to appropriate committee), Declined with feedback, or Appealed. No suggestion disappears without explanation.

E

Principle Three

Equity

"Only when the best interests of the whole are met, can the individual needs of each constituent group also be met."

The Equity Principle governs how financial gains from productivity improvements are measured and shared among the organization, its members, and its employees — fairly and transparently.

  • Gainsharing formula: Expense-to-Gross-Income productivity ratio
  • Quarterly distributions: 50% to CU capital, 40% to employees, 10% reserve
  • Reserve account absorbs deficit quarters
  • Distribution as % of compensation (FLSA compliant)
  • Fairness System for unfairness complaints & peer recognition
Learn More ▾

The Gainsharing Formula

The productivity ratio is Operating Expenses ÷ Gross Income (excluding expenses employees can't influence). A three-year historical average sets the baseline. Each quarter, if actual expenses are lower than what the baseline ratio would predict given actual gross income, a gain is generated.

Distribution Example

Using a sample gain of $100,000: $50,000 goes to the Credit Union (capital), $40,000 is distributed to eligible employees as a percentage of their compensation, and $10,000 goes to a reserve account to offset future deficit quarters.

Eligibility

Full-time, part-time, temp-to-hire, and Investment/Insurance/Travel department employees are all eligible from day one — except temporary employees on assignment.

Formula Review

The formula is reviewed every six months for the first two years, then annually. Unusual circumstances can trigger an adjustment review by the EPIC Plan Evaluation Committee.

Fairness System

A 3–5 member Fairness Committee (led by the EPIC Coordinator) reviews requests from employees who feel treated unfairly or wish to recognize exceptional colleague performance. All decisions are communicated in writing with a detailed explanation.

C

Principle Four

Competence

"Competence is the ability to respond to the constant demand for improvement and change."

The Competence Principle ensures both the organization and individuals continuously build the skills, knowledge, and capabilities needed to make the EPIC Plan succeed and deliver high-quality service.

  • Tuition assistance ($2,500/yr full-time; prorated part-time)
  • CUES Online University — 120+ credit union–specific courses
  • 4-level internal course system (100 through 400 level)
  • 16-session Supervisor's Management & Development Program
  • Deming Quality Management Training (17 sessions)
Learn More ▾

EPIC-Specific Training Courses

  • EPIC 101 — Scanlon History & EPIC Principles Overview
  • EPIC 102 — The Suggestion System (lifecycle, business case writing, appeals)
  • EPIC 103 — Introduction to Business Literacy (financial reports, SWOT, critical measures)
  • EPIC 210 — Active Team Participation (PDCA, Conway Process Improvement)
  • EPIC 310 — Leading & Working in Teams
  • EPIC 311 — Review & Screening Committee Roles & Responsibilities

Internal Training Architecture

Courses range from 100-level broad surveys (no prerequisites) to 400-level specialized practicums. CORE job-skills training, On-the-Job Training with peer coaches, software skills, and professional development are all integrated.

Deming Philosophy

CEO Chuck Cockburn introduced Deming Quality Management in 2001. The 17-session program covers all 14 Deming principles and anchors the organizational culture that EPIC builds upon — quality, data-driven decisions, and elimination of waste.

Senior Leadership Development

A dedicated pipeline identifies leaders, provides 360-degree evaluations, developmental assignments, and promotes from within — treating leadership development as a long-term organizational investment.

This plan was designed in 2005 by an elected and appointed Design Team at Watermark Credit Union (Seattle, WA), formerly Seattle Telco Federal Credit Union. It was submitted for employee ratification on October 10, 2005, requiring at least 80% staff approval to take effect. It represents an authentic, complete Scanlon Plan adapted for a growing financial services cooperative.
