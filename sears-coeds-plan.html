Section I

Identity & History

Sears Licensed Businesses are specialty businesses managed through contracts with other companies — known as Licensees — to market goods or services which complement Sears' strategic direction, in mall or off-mall locations.

🏬
Making Sears A Compelling Place to Shop
Customers require the right merchandise and services, and associates who deliver excellent customer service, with a high level of integrity and trust. We build lasting relationships that make Sears their first choice.
👥
Making Sears A Compelling Place to Work
Associates require an environment for achievement of personal goals, personal development, empowerment and involvement, while being encouraged to contribute new ideas. We treat associates fairly, in mutual dignity and respect.
📈
Making Sears A Compelling Place to Invest
Investors require revenue growth, improvements in operating margin, asset management, and increased productivity. We will act as owners of our business to deliver growth and shareholder return.
1940s
First licensed business — a key shop in Cleveland, Ohio with Cole Key Company. The concept quickly proved mutually beneficial.
1960s
Portrait Studio introduced with CPI Corporation — pioneering the concept of permanent studios inside a retail store environment.
1992
Peak: Sears licensing over 100 businesses with more than 3,000 individual contracts across the nation.
1995
Strategic refocus: Businesses reduced from 105 to 49; contracts from 3,000+ to 1,300 — aligning to core Sears strategy.
Section II

Learning & Communication

Continuous learning and open and honest communication are essential ingredients in an environment that requires full participation of all Team Members. Learning builds competency; communication creates understanding.

01 — OBJECTIVE
Two-Way Communication
Develop effective two-way communication skills, enhance problem solving, and establish an environment which welcomes diverse ideas and opinions across all team members.
02 — OBJECTIVE
Elevate Competency
Elevate the competency level of each team member through structured Personal Development courses — a minimum of three per calendar year, from Sears University or outside courses.
03 — OBJECTIVE
Business Literacy
Achieve a high level of literacy relative to our own business and the total company — ensuring every associate understands how their role contributes to company-wide performance.
04 — OBJECTIVE
High Performance Team Skills
Build the team skills necessary to function as a high performance organization — leveraging interpersonal skills, trust, and an appreciation for the diversity of the team.

Section III

Involvement

We maintain an environment that promotes an open exchange of ideas and opinions through active participation — recognizing all team members as integral contributors to the success of the department.

01
Verbal Suggestions
Ideas and solutions communicated directly to an Involvement Committee Member or the individual responsible for the area to which the suggestion pertains. Ideal for easily and inexpensively executed ideas.
02
Written Suggestion Process
A suggestion box is maintained in the department. Each submission should include a recommendation for implementing the suggestion and the desired outcome. The Involvement Committee reviews all written suggestions.
03
Action Team Formation
When a departmental opportunity is identified that falls outside the identifier's area of expertise or control, an Action Team may be formed. Everyone within the department is encouraged to lead an Action Team.
04
Final Resolution
The Vice President & General Manager holds final decision authority on all suggestions. Final resolution of Action Team work is communicated to the entire Licensed Business Department.

Section IV

Equity Plan

The Equity Plan measures and rewards team-oriented achievement of departmental goals and accountabilities. Distribution is made to each eligible associate based upon hours worked each quarter.

Key Component #1

Income Growth

The most important department goal. Income Growth is measured quarterly and annually. An Annual Award Payout Reserve Fund is calculated at 1% for each dollar of growth over a 5% income increase threshold vs. 1996.

No cap on upside · Annual payout only
Key Component #2

Business Imperatives

Each quarter features specific goals targeting outstanding performance. A quarterly Business Imperative directly impacts achievement of overall departmental goals — tied to timely business events.

Maximum payout: $40,000 annually
Key Component #3

Customer Service

Committed to ensuring customers are highly satisfied with products and services delivered by licensees. Measured through quarterly Shop & Check and telephone surveys — CSSP/60 Second Survey.

Maximum payout: $24,000 annually

The Foundation

The Essence of Team COEDS

According to Greek Mythology, the Altus was a bird which set itself apart by flying the highest of all. Team COEDS commits to achieve the highest goals in the company.

🎯
Understand Goalsharing Each Team Member commits to understand and embrace the principles of goalsharing as a framework for winning together.
📚
Continual Improvement Each Team Member commits to continually improve their personal skills and competencies through ongoing education and development.
🤝
Contribute Through Participation Each Team Member commits to contribute through active participation — bringing ideas, challenges, and solutions forward.
👁
Welcome Others' Contributions Each Team Member commits to be aware of and welcome the potential contributions of others — recognizing that all perspectives add value.
🌱
Help Others Succeed Each Team Member commits to be willing to help others contribute so that all can benefit from the shared rewards of team growth.
🏆
Performance Leadership Each Team Member commits to performance leadership that benefits our customers, associates, and shareholders — raising the standard.
